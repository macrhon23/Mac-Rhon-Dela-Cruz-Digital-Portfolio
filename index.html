4. Case Study Analyses

4.1 Summary of the Incident

The 2023 MOVEit data breach involved a zero-day vulnerability in MOVEit Transfer software, exploited by the Clop ransomware gang. Over 2,000 organizations, including healthcare and financial institutions, were affected, exposing millions of personal records and disrupting critical services.

4.2 Ethical Issues Involved

The breach raised ethical concerns about inadequate software security, failure to protect sensitive user data, and the moral implications of paying ransoms, which could perpetuate cybercrime. It also highlighted accountability gaps in third-party software vendors.

4.3 Application of Ethical Theories

Deontology suggests a duty to avoid paying ransoms, as it fuels criminal activity, and to prioritize secure software development. Virtue Ethics emphasizes the need for integrity in promptly disclosing breaches and compassion in supporting affected users, rather than concealing vulnerabilities.

4.4 Proposed Ethical Solutions and Interventions

To prevent similar incidents, organizations should adopt regular security audits, patch management, and zero-trust architectures. Ethical interventions include transparent breach disclosures, stricter vendor accountability, and advocating for laws that deter ransom payments.
